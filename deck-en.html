Saimaa Islands creates a connected network of private island destinations on Lake Saimaa — a place where families make memories, outdoor enthusiasts find their edge, and anyone seeking real Finnish nature can breathe.

Smaller islands offer exclusive glamping and private stays — just you, the forest, and the lake. Two larger hub islands provide full-service operations: guided activities, catering, and sauna facilities for school camps, retreats, and family groups.

In summer, paddle island-to-island by kayak. In winter, ski lodge-to-lodge across the frozen lake. Finland's most iconic nature experience — owned, operated, and tailored by people who have lived here for 30 years.

Kaarnetsaari —
Balcony to Pristine Nature

A church-owned island in Rantasalmi, adjacent to Linnansaari National Park — going to public sale in spring 2026. For decades the Rantasalmi congregation operated it as a camp centre. Järvisydän Resort has been the tenant and knows the island well.

The built area (~3 ha) up for sale includes: a log camp centre with dining hall and accommodation, the beautiful Kaunis Valo chapel built from island-felled pine, sauna, dock, and a 1.5 km nature trail. Ready-to-use infrastructure — no building from scratch.

As the anchor hub of the Saimaa Islands network, Kaarnetsaari handles arrivals, catering, and equipping guests for island routes. Maximum bid reserved: 400,000 €.

Glamping —
The Smart Start

Glamping is the fastest-growing segment in European nature tourism — and it's the perfect first product for Saimaa Islands. Canvas lodges and bell tents require minimal investment, can be deployed season by season, and generate premium revenue from day one.

Why glamping fits perfectly:
No permanent construction — tents are movable, scalable, and require no planning permits for seasonal use. A fully equipped canvas lodge on a private Finnish island commands €120–300 per night and books out months in advance through nature tourism platforms.

Kaarnetsaari and Iso-Virkasaari are the ideal locations: Kaarnetsaari already has kitchen, sauna, and dining infrastructure to support glamping guests. Iso-Virkasaari has space for a full glamping village with lake views on all sides.

Year 1: 5–10 tents. Year 3: 20–30 tents across the network. Low cost, high margin, high demand.

Low setup cost 120–300€/night Scalable Season 1 revenue
